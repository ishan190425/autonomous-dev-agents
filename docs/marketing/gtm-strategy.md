## Executive Summary

ADA launches as an open-source CLI tool, targeting developer early adopters who want to experiment with autonomous AI dev teams. This GTM strategy defines our phased approach from soft launch to community growth.

**Goal:** 1,000 npm downloads and 100 GitHub stars within 30 days of launch.

---

## Target Audience

### Primary Persona: The Automation Pioneer

- **Who:** Solo developers, indie hackers, startup CTOs
- **Pain:** Juggling too many contexts, AI tools are helpful but not autonomous
- **Desire:** "Set it and forget it" development automation
- **Behavior:** Active on GitHub, reads HN/dev Twitter, tries new tools early
- **Adoption trigger:** Sees demo ‚Üí "Holy shit, I want this for my project"

### Secondary Persona: The AI Curious Engineer

- **Who:** Engineers at small-mid companies (5-50 devs)
- **Pain:** Interested in AI agents but hasn't found practical use case
- **Desire:** Real productivity gains, not just "copilot autocomplete"
- **Behavior:** Follows AI research, skeptical of hype, wants to try before buy
- **Adoption trigger:** Reads technical breakdown ‚Üí "This actually makes sense"

### Tertiary Persona: The Tool Evaluator

- **Who:** Engineering managers, DevOps leads
- **Pain:** Evaluating AI tools for team adoption
- **Desire:** Clear ROI, easy to pilot, doesn't disrupt existing workflow
- **Behavior:** Reads comparison articles, wants enterprise features roadmap
- **Adoption trigger:** See public roadmap + team demo ‚Üí "Let's pilot this"
